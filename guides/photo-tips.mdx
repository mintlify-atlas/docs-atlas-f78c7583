The quality of your Simpsonized portrait starts with the photo you upload. Follow these tips to ensure your input photo gives you the best possible results.

## Photo requirements

Simpsonize Me! accepts the following formats:

- **JPG/JPEG**
- **PNG**
- **WebP**
- **HEIC** (iPhone format)
- **Maximum file size**: 15MB

<Tip>
Most modern smartphone photos work perfectly. You don't need professional photography equipment to get great results.
</Tip>

## Lighting best practices

Good lighting is crucial for facial recognition and feature detection.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use natural light when possible">
    Natural daylight provides even, flattering illumination that helps the AI detect facial features accurately. Position yourself near a window or outdoors in indirect sunlight.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Avoid harsh shadows">
    Direct overhead lighting or strong side lighting creates shadows that obscure facial features. The AI works best with even lighting across your face.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Don't backlight your photo">
    Avoid taking photos with bright light sources (windows, sun) behind you. This creates silhouettes that hide your features.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check for glare">
    If you wear glasses, adjust your angle to minimize glare and reflections that can obscure your eyes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Face visibility and composition

The AI needs to clearly see your facial features to create an accurate Simpsonized portrait.

### Head position

- **Face the camera directly** or at a slight angle (3/4 view)
- Keep your head upright and level
- Avoid extreme angles or tilted poses
- Don't crop too tightly—include some space around your head

### Facial features

<Warning>
Make sure your face is fully visible. Avoid photos where hands, objects, or hair cover significant portions of your face.
</Warning>

- **Eyes**: Both eyes should be clearly visible and open
- **Hair**: Pulled-back or styled away from the face often works best, but natural hairstyles are fine
- **Accessories**: Hats, sunglasses, or masks that cover facial features should be removed
- **Expression**: Natural smiles or neutral expressions work best

## Photo clarity and resolution

Sharp, clear photos produce the best Simpsonized results.

### Resolution guidelines

- **Minimum**: 500 x 500 pixels
- **Recommended**: 1000 x 1000 pixels or higher
- **Sweet spot**: Modern smartphone photos (typically 8MP+) are ideal

<Info>
Higher resolution input photos generally produce better output, but you don't need professional camera equipment. A recent smartphone photo is perfect.
</Info>

### Avoid these issues

- **Blurry photos**: Motion blur or out-of-focus images
- **Low light**: Grainy, noisy photos from dark environments
- **Heavy filters**: Instagram filters or Snapchat effects can confuse the AI
- **Over-editing**: Highly edited or smoothed photos may lose important facial details

## Distance and framing

How you frame your shot matters for optimal results.

<Accordion title="Portrait shots (recommended)">
Head and shoulders compositions work best. The face should occupy roughly 60-80% of the frame, with some space around the head.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Full body shots">
While the AI can work with full-body photos, your face will be smaller in the frame, potentially reducing accuracy. If using a full-body shot, ensure your face is still clearly visible and well-lit.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Group photos">
Simpsonize Me! works with group photos! Just make sure each person's face is clearly visible and well-lit. You'll get variations featuring everyone in the shot Simpsonized together.
</Accordion>

## Background considerations

The background of your photo affects how well the AI can isolate your features.

- **Simple backgrounds** work best (solid colors, plain walls)
- **Uncluttered scenes** help the AI focus on faces
- **Avoid busy patterns** that might distract from facial features
- **Outdoor backgrounds** are fine if your face is well-lit and in focus

<Tip>
You don't need a professional backdrop. A simple wall at home works perfectly!
</Tip>

## Special cases

### Photos with pets

Yes! You can Simpsonize your pets too. The same principles apply:

- Clear, well-lit photo of your pet's face
- Pet should be looking toward the camera
- Avoid motion blur from movement
- Works great for dogs, cats, and other pets

### Multiple people

Group photos work with Simpsonize Me! For best results:

- Ensure everyone's face is clearly visible
- All faces should be roughly the same distance from the camera
- Good, even lighting across all subjects
- Avoid people overlapping or partially obscuring each other

### Selfies

Selfies work great! Just remember:

- Use your front camera for easier framing
- Hold the camera at arm's length or use a selfie stick
- Avoid extreme close-ups
- Check the lighting before taking the shot

## Quick checklist

Before uploading your photo, verify:

- ✅ Face is clearly visible and well-lit
- ✅ Photo is sharp and in focus
- ✅ No heavy filters or excessive editing
- ✅ File format is JPG, PNG, WebP, or HEIC
- ✅ File size is under 15MB
- ✅ Good contrast between face and background
- ✅ Natural expression (smile or neutral)

<Info>
Remember: You'll receive 5 unique variations of your Simpsonized portrait, so the AI has multiple chances to capture your likeness perfectly!
</Info>
