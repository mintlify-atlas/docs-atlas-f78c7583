### Aspect ratio considerations

Your Simpsonized portrait's aspect ratio determines which print sizes work best without cropping:

- **Square prints**: 8x8", 10x10", 12x12"
- **Standard prints**: 4x6", 5x7", 8x10"
- **Custom prints**: Match the original aspect ratio for no cropping

<Warning>
When ordering prints, select "no crop" or "fit to frame" options to ensure you don't lose any part of your Simpsonized portrait.
</Warning>

## Print types and materials

Different print types create different looks and experiences for your Simpsonized portrait.

### Standard photo prints

Traditional photo paper prints:

- **Glossy finish**: Vibrant colors, reflective surface
- **Matte finish**: No glare, modern look
- **Lustre/satin finish**: Best of both—vibrant with minimal glare

<Tip>
Lustre or satin finish is recommended for Simpsonized portraits. It provides vibrant colors without the glare of glossy prints.
</Tip>

### Canvas prints

Canvas-wrapped prints create gallery-quality wall art:

- **Museum quality**: Archival canvas and inks
- **Stretched and wrapped**: Ready to hang, no frame needed
- **Textured surface**: Artistic, painting-like appearance
- **Sizes**: From 8x10" to 30x40" and larger

<Info>
Canvas prints are perfect for creating statement pieces. The Simpsons art style translates beautifully to canvas!
</Info>

### Metal prints

Modern, durable aluminum prints:

- **Vibrant colors**: Exceptionally bright and bold
- **Glossy finish**: High-impact visual appeal
- **Durable**: Scratch-resistant and waterproof
- **Contemporary**: Modern, frameless look

### Acrylic prints

Premium acrylic face-mounted prints:

- **Depth and dimension**: 3D-like appearance
- **Brilliant colors**: Exceptional vibrancy and clarity
- **Luxury presentation**: High-end gallery quality
- **Most expensive**: Premium pricing for premium results

### Poster prints

Affordable large-format prints:

- **Budget-friendly**: Great for large sizes
- **Lightweight**: Easy to hang and move
- **Requires framing**: Looks best in a frame or poster holder

## Uploading your image for printing

When you're ready to order prints, follow these steps for best results.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download your images">
    Download all 5 variations from the email link you received. Choose your favorite for printing, or print multiple versions.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review on your computer">
    View the full-resolution image on your computer to ensure it looks exactly how you want it before ordering prints.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Upload to printing service">
    Upload the original high-resolution file—do not edit, resize, or compress the image before uploading.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select print specifications">
    Choose your desired print size, finish, and quantity. Select "no crop" or "full bleed" options when available.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Preview before ordering">
    Use the service's preview feature to see how your print will look. Check that nothing important is cropped.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete your order">
    Review shipping time and costs, then complete your order. Save your tracking information.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Always upload the original file you received from Simpsonize Me! Do not screenshot, compress, or edit the image before uploading for printing.
</Warning>

## Print quality tips

### Ensuring the best results

- **Use the original file**: Upload the unmodified high-resolution file
- **Choose quality settings**: Select highest quality printing options
- **Select proper paper**: Use photo paper for standard prints
- **Avoid compression**: Don't email or text the file (use direct upload)
- **Check previews**: Review the preview before finalizing your order

### Color accuracy

<Info>
The bright yellow of Simpsons characters may appear slightly different on different monitors and prints. This is normal and part of the printing process.
</Info>

- Colors look different on screen vs. print (this is normal)
- Glossy prints appear more vibrant than matte
- Different paper types affect color appearance
- Professional labs offer the most accurate color reproduction

## Troubleshooting print issues

### Common problems and solutions

<Accordion title="Print looks blurry or pixelated">
**Cause**: File may have been compressed or resized  
**Solution**: Re-download the original file from your email and upload without modifications
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Colors look different than on screen">
**Cause**: Normal variation between screen and print  
**Solution**: This is expected. If very different, try a different printing service or paper type
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Image is cropped incorrectly">
**Cause**: Wrong crop settings during ordering  
**Solution**: Reorder with "no crop" or "fit to frame" selected. Some services offer free reprints for errors
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Print appears too dark or too light">
**Cause**: Printer calibration or paper type  
**Solution**: Try a different paper finish or contact the printing service for color correction
</Accordion>

### When to reorder

Most quality printing services offer satisfaction guarantees:

- **Printing errors**: Free replacement for printer mistakes
- **Damaged in shipping**: Free reprint or refund
- **Color issues**: Many services will adjust and reprint
- **Wrong size**: Typically replaced at no charge

<Tip>
Take photos of any print quality issues before contacting customer service. This helps resolve the issue quickly.
</Tip>
